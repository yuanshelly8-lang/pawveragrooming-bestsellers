OEM vs. ODM: What's the Difference?

OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) means you take an existing clipper design from a factory and put your brand on it. This is the fastest and most capital-efficient route—typical lead times run 25–35 days.

ODM (Original Design Manufacturer) means the factory designs and produces a product to your specification. This gives you a truly unique SKU, but requires more upfront investment in tooling fees, sampling, and a longer 60–90 day lead time.

For most first-time private label buyers, OEM is the recommended starting point. You can validate demand with lower risk, then invest in ODM on your best-performing SKU.

What Can Be Customized in an OEM Pet Clipper?

  • Logo placement: Laser engraving on the clipper body (most durable), UV printing, or debossed logo on the grip panel.
  • Color: Most factories support 3–5 standard color options with no 500+ unit orders.
  • Packaging: Full-color custom retail box design. We recommend including your logo, a key spec comparison table, and QR codes linking to your warranty page.
  • Accessories: Choose your comb guide set, add a premium oil bottle or a cleaning brush with your logo.
  • Manual: Fully rewritten in your brand voice and target language (Dutch, German, English, etc.).

Key Red Flags When Choosing a Supplier

  • Cannot provide original CE or FCC test certificates (only screenshots or PDFs that look self-produced).
  • Unwilling to send a production sample before bulk shipment—walk away immediately.
  • No existing export history to the EU or US (check their Alibaba trade activity).
  • Quotes DDP pricing without confirming HS code and destination duty rates.
