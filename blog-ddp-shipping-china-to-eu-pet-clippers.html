DDP Shipping from China to EU for Pet Clippers: A Practical Importer's Guide

DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) shipping eliminates most of the complexity of importing from China. Here's what EU buyers sourcing pet clippers need to understand before placing their first wholesale order.

What DDP Actually Means — and What It Doesn't

Under Incoterms 2020, DDP means the seller is responsible for all costs and risks until the goods are delivered to the named destination — including export customs clearance, international freight, insurance, import customs clearance, and all import duties and taxes in the destination country.

For buyers in the Netherlands, Germany, France, or UK, this means you pay one price and goods arrive at your warehouse ready to use. You do not deal with freight forwarders, customs agents, or duty payments. This is why DDP is increasingly the preferred Incoterm for first-time importers and for buyers who want to simplify their supply chain.

What DDP does not cover: unloading charges at the buyer's premises (unless specifically agreed), and any country-specific compliance testing that may be required after import (for example, if a bol.com audit requests additional documentation after your products are already in the country).

Import Duties on Pet Clippers into the EU

Pet clippers (electric hair clippers and shavers) typically fall under HS Code 8510.20 — "Hair clippers." The EU standard import duty rate for this category is 2.7% of the customs value (CIF value: cost + insurance + freight).

On top of the duty, EU member states charge VAT at their domestic rate:

  • Netherlands: 21%
  • Germany: 19%
  • France: 20%
  • Belgium: 21%
  • Sweden: 25%
  • Norway: 25% (not EU, but applies same HS logic)

Under DDP, your supplier handles all of these payments and includes them in the per-unit price. When comparing DDP prices from different suppliers, confirm whether their DDP quote includes VAT — some suppliers quote DDP excluding VAT (sometimes called "DDU + duties"), which is technically a different Incoterm.

Lead Times: Sea vs Air Freight

For EU destinations, sea freight from Hangzhou typically runs 30–35 days via the main container ports (Rotterdam, Hamburg, Antwerp). This is the cost-effective option for orders above approximately 200kg. Air freight runs 8–12 days and is used for smaller quantities, urgency orders, or first sample shipments.

Lithium Battery Rules for Air Freight

Pet clippers contain lithium-ion batteries, which are classified as dangerous goods for air transport under IATA regulations. To ship by air legally, the battery must carry:

  • UN38.3 certification — proof that the battery has passed the UN transport test series
  • MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheet) — required by airlines and customs authorities
  • Proper labelling on outer cartons (Class 9 miscellaneous dangerous goods label)

What Documentation to Expect

A complete DDP shipment should arrive with:

  • Commercial Invoice (for your accounting records)
  • Packing List (carton-level breakdown)
  • Bill of Lading or Air Waybill
  • UN38.3 + MSDS for the battery
  • CE Declaration of Conformity (for EU destinations)
  • Certificate of Origin

All documents are provided digitally before the goods arrive and originals are included with the shipment where required by destination customs.

Tip for first-time importers: Even under DDP, you will receive customs clearance documents from your freight forwarder. These are for your records only — you do not need to take any action.
