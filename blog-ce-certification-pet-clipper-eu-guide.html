CE Certification for Pet Clippers: Complete EU Compliance Guide

Understanding CE certification is one of the most important steps for any importer or brand owner sourcing pet clippers for the EU market. This guide explains what CE means, what it covers, and what to ask your supplier.

What Is CE Certification and Why Does It Matter?

CE marking is a mandatory conformity mark for products sold in the European Economic Area (EEA). For pet clippers, CE certification confirms that the product complies with relevant EU safety and electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) directives. Without it, a product cannot legally be placed on the EU market — and platforms like bol.com and Amazon EU routinely audit sellers and delist non-compliant listings.

The most relevant directive for cordless pet clippers is the EMC Directive 2014/30/EU, which governs electromagnetic emissions and immunity. Products with lithium batteries also fall under the Battery Directive.

CE Certification vs. Self-Declaration: What's the Difference?

There are two common routes to CE marking for low-voltage consumer electronics:

  • Self-Declaration (DoC): The manufacturer issues a Declaration of Conformity without third-party testing. This is legally permitted for certain product categories, but carries higher risk and is increasingly rejected by major retailers and marketplaces.
  • Third-Party Testing: An accredited lab (SGS, TÜV Rheinland, Intertek, LCS, etc.) independently tests the product and issues a test report. The manufacturer then issues the DoC referencing these results. This is the gold standard buyers should require.

When evaluating a wholesale pet clipper supplier, always ask for the test report number, the accredited lab name, and the issue date. A certificate issued before 2018 may not cover current EN standards and should be refreshed.

What Standards Apply to Pet Clippers for EU Sale?

  • EN 55014-1:2017 — Electromagnetic disturbance from household appliances (emissions)
  • EN 55014-2:2015 — Immunity requirements for household appliances
  • EN 61000-3-2:2014 — Limits for harmonic current emissions
  • EN 61000-3-3:2013 — Voltage fluctuations and flicker
  • EU Battery Directive 2006/66/EC — For lithium-ion battery products

RoHS: The Other Required Compliance

In addition to EMC, pet clippers sold in the EU must comply with the RoHS Directive (2011/65/EU), which restricts hazardous substances including lead, mercury, cadmium, and certain flame retardants. RoHS compliance should be confirmed via a supplier declaration or third-party test report. For OEM and private label orders, confirm RoHS compliance before production.

What to Ask Your Supplier Before Placing an Order

  • Which test lab issued the CE certificate? Can you share the reference number and the full test report?
  • Does the certificate cover the exact model I am ordering, or a similar model?
  • Is the battery UN38.3 certified for air and sea freight? Can you provide the MSDS?
  • Is your Declaration of Conformity (DoC) available in English?
  • Has the certificate been renewed in the last 3 years?
